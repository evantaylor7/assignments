Record Snowfall Hits Salt Lake City

by Evan Taylor

Never has this much snow been seen in the valley and mountains of Salt Lake City. Pictured left is, in fact, the city of Salt Lake. It is unrecognizable to most that know it, however, due to the fact that there is so much snow that buildings cannot be seen. Ski and snowboard enthusiasts were thrilled until they realized that all roads going up to the mountains were indefinitely closed. How much snow exactly fell you ask? 214 inches (17.83 feet). Unheard of.

Scientists link this record snowfall to global climate change, even though the democratic conspiracy theory is usually tied to warmer weather instead of the cold-birthed solid state of water.

"Whether or not this phenomena is due to me driving a car to work every day and cow farts, legit skiiers - and a very small percentage of snowboarders who decided to put down the bong and get off the couch - are backcountry skiing the foothills directly east of the city, if not heading up to further heights on snowshoes, ski skins, and snowmobiles," reported some guy on the street I just met.
